A Study about Statements and Opinions

Instructions

Thank you for participating in this study! Please read the instructions carefully.

In this study, you will be shown a statement. First, please decide whether you agree or disagree with the statement. (Note that there is no right or wrong answer for your opinions.)

And then, you will be shown two sets of opinions and will be asked to select which set of the opinions are more diverse.

Two sets of opinions can be written in different structure but they can still convey the same idea.

Set ASet B
  • I love to eat ice cream on a warm summer day.
  • Eating ice cream feels great when it's so hot outside.
  • I eat ice cream when I am sad because its sweetness can make me happy again.
  • I like ice cream.
  • I love ice cream.
  • Eating ice cream is always fun.

For example, Set A and B convey the same idea about why they like eating ice cream, but the contents are different. If you think the opinions in the Set A convey more diverse perspectives, then choose A . If you think B sounds more diverse opinions, then choose B.

You will have 1 practice session. In total there will be 5 sets of statements and their related opinions.
